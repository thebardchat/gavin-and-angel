In their own words

How We Met

We should have connected sooner than we did.

In 2021, when Angel was in law school, several of Gavin's friends visited Tuscaloosa — Gavin had other plans and had to miss the trip.

In May 2023, we were both in a wedding for Alyssa and Cody Cook. We chatted briefly but nothing of substance.

It wasn't until September 2023 when we officially talked for the first time. While Gavin and Alyssa were at a wedding shower, Alyssa was convinced that she had a match for Gavin. Not even a full hour later, Gavin texted Angel for the first time.

Gavin made the first big move, inviting Angel to his birthday celebration in Huntsville. The two of us talked for three hours before Angel had to go. Gavin once again made a big move, asking Angel to go on another date in Birmingham the next week. We have been together ever since.

From our “medium distance” relationship to officially making Birmingham our home, we have been inseparable (even when Tennessee plays Alabama). And we can't wait for what is to come.

So yeah, we should have connected sooner than we did. But we connected exactly when we were supposed to.

— A + G

A letter from Shane

To Gavin & Angel

To my first-born and the woman he chose —

Gavin, I watched you grow up. From Hampton Cove to Tuscaloosa — Roll Tide — to Birmingham, to wherever you and Angel decide to go from here, I have had a front-row seat to the man you've become.

Angel, you walked into a big family — five sons, plus Gavin's sister — and made the oldest brother whole. You're the lawyer. You're the matchmaker's match. You're a daughter now, and there's no provisional clause in that — you're in.

Whatever we built before today gets handed to you both now. Faith, family, the hard-earned kind of clarity that doesn't come without a price. Take all of it. Build your own version. Add to it. Hand it down.

And Jace — son of my son, the only black lab/pit ever listed in a wedding party as “Our Son” — I see you too. Stop stealing Michael Avery's snacks.
